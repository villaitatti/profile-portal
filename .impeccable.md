## Design Context

### Users
Mix of Harvard I Tatti staff (doing quick admin tasks — managing fellows, apps, Atlassian sync) and research fellows/appointees (checking their profile, accessing internal apps, claiming VIT ID credentials). Both groups want speed and clarity. Staff are repeat power users; fellows may visit infrequently and need intuitive navigation.

### Brand Personality
Warm, approachable, reliable. Friendly and inviting despite being institutional, the kind of tool that feels helpful, not bureaucratic. Three words: warm, clear, trustworthy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light theme appropriate for daytime office use at a Renaissance research institute in Florence. Cool-toned institutional greys (derived from brand light grey #e5eaec and anthracite #1d252c) with restrained crimson accents (#ab192d). Content area capped at max-w-6xl to prevent stretch on wide displays. Typography stays in Brandon Grotesque, leaning on its humanist warmth to balance the cooler surface palette.

### Design Principles
- Let content outrank chrome. Navigation should support the task, not dominate it.
- Clarity first. Every element should be immediately understandable, with no jargon or ambiguity.
- Favor readable body copy and clear hierarchy over tiny metadata.
- Warm professionalism. Institutional without being cold, approachable without being casual.
- Efficient by default. Respect users' time, minimize clicks, maximize scannability.
- Quiet confidence. Use accent color with restraint, avoid loud glows and heavy nav treatments.
- Prefer consistent shared patterns over one-off styling.
