## Design Context

### Users
Mix of Harvard I Tatti staff (doing quick admin tasks — managing fellows, apps, Atlassian sync) and research fellows/appointees (checking their profile, accessing internal apps, claiming VIT ID credentials). Both groups want speed and clarity. Staff are repeat power users; fellows may visit infrequently and need intuitive navigation.

### Brand Personality
Warm, approachable, reliable. Friendly and inviting despite being institutional — the kind of tool that feels helpful, not bureaucratic. Three words: **warm, clear, trustworthy**.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Light theme appropriate for daytime office use at a Renaissance research institute in Florence
- Brand colors: Crimson (#ab192d), Anthracite (#1d252c), Light grey (#e5eaec)
- Typography: Brandon Grotesque (already in use) — humanist sans-serif that balances warmth with professionalism
- No specific external references; aim for professional quality that doesn't feel like a generic university portal
- Should feel like a well-made internal tool, not a marketing site

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity first** — Every element should be immediately understandable. No jargon, no ambiguity.
2. **Warm professionalism** — Institutional without being cold. Approachable without being casual.
3. **Efficient by default** — Respect users' time. Minimize clicks, maximize scannability.
4. **Quiet confidence** — The design should feel intentional and considered, not flashy or trendy.
